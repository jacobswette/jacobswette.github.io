Introduction

About

As a researcher, I study how human behaviors drive environmental change and, in turn, shape health across species and across the human life course. My research focuses on climate-related shifts in vector-borne disease distribution; chronic pain in children as an outcome of social and environmental stress; and potential neoplastic disease pathways associated with micro- and nanoplastic exposures.

My training background is highly interdisciplinary, spanning the health sciences, statistics, biomimicry, and philosophy. These disciplines inform how I frame research questions and approach complex health problems.
