Introduction

About

I am an MPH Epidemiology student at the University of South Florida College of Public Health, with concurrent graduate certificates in Applied Biostatistics and Epidemiology of Infectious Disease. My research interests center on daily and seasonal behavioral rhythms in human health, nature exposure and ecological determinants of well-being, and One Health and planetary health pathways through which environmental change shapes disease ecology across species.

My path to epidemiology has been deeply interdisciplinary. I hold an MS in Biomimicry from Arizona State University (graduated with distinction), a Doctor of Acupuncture from Pacific College of Health and Science, and a BS in Interdisciplinary Studies with a focus on health epistemology from the University of Wisconsin–Superior. I have spent over a decade in clinical practice as a licensed acupuncturist and integrative health team leader, and I bring that frontline perspective to my research in population health.
