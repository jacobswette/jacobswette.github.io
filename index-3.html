Introduction

About

I am an MPH Epidemiology student at the University of South Florida College of Public Health, with concurrent graduate certificates in Applied Biostatistics and Epidemiology of Infectious Disease. My research interests center on daily and seasonal behavioral rhythms in human health, nature exposure and ecological determinants of well-being, and One Health and planetary health pathways through which environmental change shapes disease ecology across species.

My path to epidemiology has been deeply interdisciplinary. I hold an MS in Biomimicry from Arizona State University (graduated with distinction) and a BS in Interdisciplinary Studies with a focus on health epistemology from the University of Wisconsin–Superior. I bring over a decade of clinical healthcare experience and an interdisciplinary perspective to my research in population health.

Biomimicry

I apply Life's Principles and ecological design thinking to challenges at the intersection of human health and the natural world.
